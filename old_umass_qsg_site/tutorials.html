QSG Tutorials

QSG is developing a series of tutorials on statistical modeling in R to aid ECo students and faculty in the use of R for common statistical modeling applications in ecology and environmental conservation. Each tutorial comes complete with all the necessary datasets and R script.

DISCLAIMER -- These tutorials are "take it or leave it". Importantly, these tutorials have not undergone any external critical review, e.g., by trained statisticians. Thus, we cannot stand behind the accuracy of 100% of the material presented in these tutorials. Moreover, these tutorials are being provided in the public domain, and the recipient may not assert any proprietary rights thereto nor represent it to anyone as other than a UMass Environmental Conservation Quantitative Sciences Group publication.

What's Included in a Tutoral

Each tutorial zip file includes a pdf document (the tutorial), any required dataset(s), and the associated R script. Although the tutorials cover a wide variety of topics and have been developed by different individuals, we have tried to adopt the following organization, as appropriate, for each topic:

  • Summary -- bullet list of distinguishing characteristics/features of the topic
  • Background -- very brief background discussion on the topic
  • Packages and data -- list of the R packages need and a description of the example dataset
  • Data screening and evaluation -- procedures for screening and evaluating the dataset
  • Model fitting -- procedures for fitting the statistical model (i.e., estimating model parameters)
  • Model evaluation -- procedures for evaluating the model specification
  • Significance tests -- procedures for hypothesis testing (e.g., significance of model parameters)
  • Variable/model selection -- procedures for selecting the "best" set of predictors and/or comparing alternative models
  • Model prediction -- procedures for making predictions from the fitted model
  • Model validation -- procedures to validating the model; i.e., determining its reliability

Note, not all topics lend themselves to the outline above and thus each tutorial is organized as appropriate for the topic.

Feedback on Tutorials

As disclaimed above, these tutorials are being provided on a peer-to-peer basis and are not guaranteed to be comprehensive or 100% accurate. Consequently, in the spirit of open-source sharing, we encourage your feedback on the tutorials. Please feel free to share with us your comments on the tutorials, e.g., where clarification is needed or where alternative and/or additional procedures could enhance the treatment of the topic. And please provide the recommended R script for any changes and additions recommended. Please email your feedback to QSG.
