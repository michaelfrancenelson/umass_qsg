QSG Courses

QSG offers a four-semester sequence of courses on the analysis of environmental data, in addition to the regular periodic offerings of courses on specialized topics in spatial data analysis and systems modeling. The core courses are as follows:

  • Analysis of Environmental Data [ECO602 & ECO634] -- Taught every fall semester; 3 credit lecture (ECO602) and 2 credit optional lab (ECO634); Instructor: Dr. McGarigal. The lecture portion of this course is a required quantitative science course for all incoming MS graduate students in Environmental Conservation (ECo). This course provides an introduction and overview of foundational concepts in statistical modeling and is coupled with an optional lab section that provides hands-on training in the use of the R programming language and statistical computing environment to conduct basic statistical analyses, which is prerequisite for all subsequent stats courses offered through ECo. Restrictions: Graduate status in Environmental Conservation or Organismic and Evolutionary Biology, or consent of instructor.[click here for detailed course information]

  • Applied Biostatistics [ECO697AB] -- Taught every spring semester; 4 credits; combined lecture and lab; Instructor: Dr. Sutherland. This course provides an introduction to common univariate statistical methods in ecology and environmental conservation. Restrictions: Graduate status in Environmental Conservation or Organismic and Evolutionary Biology and Analysis of Environmental Data (ECO602 and ECO634), or consent of instructor.[click here for detailed course information]

  • Advanced Statistical Ecology [ECO697SA] -- Taught fall spring semester, even years; 4 credits; combined lecture and lab; Instructor: Dr. Finn. This course provides an introduction to advanced statistical modeling methods, including multi-level or hierarchical models and Bayesian inference methods. Restrictions: Graduate status in Environmental Conservation or Organismic and Evolutionary Biology and Analysis of Environmental Data (ECO602 and ECO634), or consent of instructor.[click here for detailed course information]

  • Applied Multivariate Statistics [ECO634] -- Taught spring semester, odd years; 4 credits; combined lecture and lab; Instructor: Dr. McGarigal. This course provides an overview of multivariate statistical methods for ecological data that couples lectures on the theory with computer lab projects to provide hands-on practical experience using the R software to conduct multivariate analyses of real ecological data sets. Students are encourage to substitute their own data sets for the ones provided to get even more practical value from the course. Restrictions: Graduate status in Environmental Conservation or Organismic and Evolutionary Biology and Analysis of Environmental Data (ECO602 and ECO634), or consent of instructor.[click here for detailed course information]
